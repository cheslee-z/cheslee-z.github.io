About me

I’m a master student at BUAA advised by Prof. Dawei Li. I have a passion for robots (especially quadcopters), and I’m committed to exploring their possible future applications. Specifically, my current research interests mainly focus on the autonomous navigation of UAVs in dynamic environments, which involves the whole process of perception, localization, mapping, planning and control. Although this sounds like a lot of work, it’s okay, because I hope to become a full-stack robotic algorithm engineer in the future.

Autonomous Navigation of UAVs in Dynamic Environments
Based on Gazebo, PX4 and ROS, we built a simulation system to verify the visual navigation function of UAV.
Longitudinal control of fixed-wing UAV based on total energy control and active disturbance rejection control
We explore the transformation between the energy states and the control actuators, and improve the basic architecture of TEC in conjunction with Linear Active Disturbance Rejection Control (LADRC) for stronger robustness. And the superiority of the designed control scheme, which is subsequently called LADRC-TEC, is demonstrated by comparison with the classical TEC scheme.
